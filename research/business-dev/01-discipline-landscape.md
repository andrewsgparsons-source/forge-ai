## The Taxonomy

### A. CORE MANAGEMENT FUNCTIONS (Fayol's Framework)
These are the *verbs* of management â€” what you DO across all disciplines:
1. **Planning** â€” forecasting, goal-setting, strategic thinking
2. **Organising** â€” structuring resources, roles, workflows
3. **Commanding/Leading** â€” directing people, motivating, decision-making
4. **Coordinating** â€” aligning activities across functions
5. **Controlling** â€” monitoring, measuring, correcting

*Every discipline below requires all five functions.*

## ERP Context â€” What Enterprise Software Already Does

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems like SAP, Oracle, and Microsoft Dynamics already try to cover most of these disciplines. Their typical modules:

- Financial (GL, AP, AR, budgeting)
- HR (payroll, recruitment, training)
- Manufacturing / Production
- Supply Chain (procurement, inventory, logistics)
- Sales & Distribution
- CRM
- Project Management
- Quality Management
- Asset Management

**The problem with ERP for small businesses:**
- Massively over-engineered (designed for 500+ employee companies)
- Expensive (Â£10k-Â£1M+ implementation)
- Rigid (you adapt to the system, not the system to you)
- Joyless (enterprise UX is famously awful)
- Overkill for a 1-10 person team

**Our opportunity:** Build something with ERP-level thinking but small-business-level simplicity. The disciplines are the same â€” a garden builder needs production management just as much as a car factory does. But the INTERFACE and COMPLEXITY must be radically different.
