## The Grounding Principle (18:35)

Andrew's correction:
> "The most important thing in all of this is to understand what we've already built so far and why it doesn't work very well and make sure that we keep checking in and heading towards something that makes sense and something that is easy and joyful to use."

**This is non-negotiable.** No amount of MBA theory matters if the thing isn't a joy to use.

### What We've Built So Far (Honest Assessment)

**1. Solution Planner (command-center)**
- Lightweight viewer — pulls data from other dashboards
- Has: Today view, Incubator (3-stage), per-dashboard drill-in, James status
- **Problem:** It's just a read-only aggregator. No real management capability. The incubator was empty until today.

**2. Garden Buildings Dashboard (shed-project-board)**
- 134 cards, Kanban columns, deliverable pages, ideas section
- **Problems:** Ideas are localStorage-only (don't sync across devices). Everything is a flat card — no hierarchy. Data fragmented between cards.json and localStorage.

**3. Whelpley Farm Dashboard**
- 6 business areas, financial data, calendar, field management
- **Problems:** Different data format from garden buildings. Ideas system is DIFFERENT AGAIN. Mobile partially broken. Calendar is localStorage.

**4. Forge AI / GrowCabin**
- Referenced in config but barely exist

### Why It Doesn't Work Well — Root Causes

1. **Three different ideas systems, none talking to each other.** Incubator, shed ideas, farm ideas — all different formats, different storage.
2. **No hierarchy.** Everything is a flat card. No projects → jobs → tasks structure. Can't see how things relate.
3. **Data fragmentation.** Some in JSON files (committed to git), some in localStorage (stuck on one device). No single source of truth.
4. **Dashboard duplication.** Each dashboard reinvents the wheel independently instead of sharing a common framework.
5. **The "lens not notebook" principle isn't being followed.** The Solution Planner was meant to be a lens into other dashboards, but the dashboards themselves are incoherent.
6. **Built for the developer, not the user.** Andrew needs something that's easy and joyful — not a developer's project board.

### The Design North Star

Whatever we build must be:
- **Easy** — not overwhelming, not technical-feeling
- **Joyful** — actually pleasant to use, not a chore
- **Coherent** — one system, one mental model, not five different apps
- **Grounded** — built from what we know doesn't work, not from theory
